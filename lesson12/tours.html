Cozumel Touring Sites

Palancar Reef

Palancar Reef is a large coral reef on the southwest side of the island of Cozumel and is part of the Arrecifes de Cozumel National Park. The site, popular with scuba divers, is divided into several sections based on depth and coral formations.

Cozumel's Beaches

Among the most popular and attractive of Cozumel's beaches are Playa San Juan and Playa Santa Pilar in the northwest corner of the island, and Playa San Francisco, Playa Santa Rosa, and Playa Palancar to the southwest.

San Miguel de Cozumel

On the northwest side of Cozumel, San Miguel de Cozumel is the island's capital and offers many interesting distractions. As a tourist hub, it's also a busy port and hosts cruise ships as they ply the sparkling waters of the Caribbean Sea.

Laguna Chankanaab

A small freshwater south of San Miguel de Cozumel in the National Marine Park. It's a popular spot for outings thanks to its crystal-clear water and numerous colorful tropical fish, and is an enchanting place to swim, snorkel, and scuba dive.

Punta Sur Eco Beach

A fun place to explore diversity of Cozumel's coastline and experience its flora and fauna. It is the largest eco park on the island with plenty of great swimming and beach fun, some snorkeling, scuba diving, and kayaking.

Dolphin Experiences

Cozumel has a number of popular dolphin experiences. One of the best is at Dolphinaris Cozumel, and offers an exciting program of dolphin swims. Another is Dolphin Discovery Cozumel, situated in Chankanaab National Park
